Cheryl Merzel, Dr.P.H., M.P.H.

Cheryl Merzel, Dr.P.H., was recently appointed Director of the new Master of Public Health program at the Institute for Public Health Sciences of Yeshiva University and is an Associate Professor in the Department of Epidemiology and Population Health, Albert Einstein College of Medicine, Yeshiva University.

Dr. Merzel received her M.P.H. and Dr.P.H. in Sociomedical Sciences from Columbia University Mailman School of Public Health. Prior to her current appointment, she served on the public health faculty of the City University of New York and Columbia University Mailman School of Public Health. She has over 20 years experience in public health research, evaluation, program development, and advocacy.

Dr. Merzel’s research interests include the design and evaluation of community-based health promotion interventions and the development of community capacity to engage in public health efforts. In the area of tobacco control, Dr. Merzel’s current research focuses on prevention of smoking resumption among postpartum women and evaluation of tobacco control advocacy networks. She was an investigator for a CDC-funded community-based participatory research project assessing capacity for tobacco control in Central Harlem. Dr. Merzel led the development of an evidence-based maternal smoking cessation intervention for the HRSA-funded Downstate New York Healthy Start project. She is an investigator for the evaluation of the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation’s Tobacco Policy Change program. She has published extensively on a variety of community-based public health topics.
