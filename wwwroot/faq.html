Q: I'm just starting a local community coalition. How can I use the information on Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T. to make my case to prospective members?

A: Cancer control planners, program staff, and researchers have the same goals: to reduce cancer risk, the number of new cancer cases, and the number of deaths from cancer, as well as enhance the quality of life for cancer survivors. While many share the same goals, all do not have easy access to resources that can facilitate the transfer of evidence-based research findings into practice. This Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T. portal provides access to data and resources that can help planners, program staff, and researchers to design, implement and evaluate evidence-based cancer control programs.

The Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T. portal provides access to Web-based resources that can assist in:

  • Assessing the cancer and/or risk factor burden within a given state.
  • Identifying potential partner organizations that may already be working with high-risk populations.
  • Understanding the current research findings and recommendations.
  • Accessing and downloading evidence-based programs and products.
  • Finding guidelines for planning and evaluation.

Q: What are some ways that I can help promote Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T.?

A: There are several ways that you can help promote Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T. A Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T. information fact sheet can be downloaded from our website and is intended for those involved in planning and developing programs (not for those who do cancer screenings). If you are interested in conducting a training or being trained on the Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T., please contact the NCI Cancer Information Service individual within your state or territory. They are listed under Program Partners within Step 2 of the Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T. web portal.

Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T. give-away items ( e.g. pens, sticky notes, etc.) are also available and can be provided to individuals who conduct trainings or otherwise promote the Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T. website. If interested, please e-mail us using the "Contact Us" link and include the following information below.

  • Name of the event you are requesting the promotional items
  • Date(s) of the event
  • Purpose of the event
  • Number of people anticipated attending the event.
  • Address for mail the items (include who they should be sent to)

There is no fee associated with using these promotion items but please note that due to limited availability, the items are reserved for individuals promoting the P.L.A.N.E.T. at a training, presentation or exhibit.

Q: Does Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T. participate in outreach workshops?

A: Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T. does not participate in outreach workshops. However, our program partners from states, tribes, and territories within the U.S. that work with community-based programs are able to help you with your outreach efforts. For further assistance, please contact program partners within your state or territory. Below is a link to contact information for all of our program partners.

http://cancercontrolplanet.cancer.gov/partners/list.jsp?r=ALL&cctopic=C

Q: How would one cite Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T. (generally)?

A: If you are citing Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T., use the following format below:

State Cancer Profiles (or any section of the portal), Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T. http://cancercontrolplanet.cancer.gov/. Accessed on DATE. Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality, American Cancer Society, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, the Commission on Cancer, National Cancer Institute, and Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration.

Q: How can I obtain a copy of the Comprehensive Cancer Control Plan for my state?

A: On Step 5 of Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T., you can view and download comprehensive cancer control plans. You can select the hyperlink: Comprehensive Cancer Control Plans. Then, click on your state, either on the text or on the map, and its map will appear. You can save it as a PDF on your computer and print out the pages that you need.

Q: A few researchers in my institution are interested in becoming research partners on Cancer Control P.L.A.N.E.T. What are the criteria for being listed as a research partner?

A: The criteria for being listed as a research partner on the website is listed below:
  1. Need to have received a grant during the fiscal year period from 1998-2009.
  2. The grant received has to have been funded by ACS, CDC, NCI, or AHRQ.
  3. Individual has permitted their contact information to be listed as a resource for comprehensive cancer control planning and implementation within their state.
If your researchers meet these criteria, please e-mail us at the "Contact Us" link with their contact information, their funding source, and their research grant number. All information will be reviewed and if eligible, an invitation letter will be sent to them with further information about being a research partner on our website.
