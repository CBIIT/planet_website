Dialogue on Dissemination: A Policy Perspective
Tuesday, October 26, 2010, 2:00–3:30 p.m. ET

Seminar Summary

Disseminating evidence-based programs and policies requires that researchers, policy makers, and practitioners take active roles in the process. While their perspectives, systems, and practices vary, their goals are the same: to reduce the burden of cancer and move cancer control “research to reality.”

This fall, the National Cancer Institute will sponsor a series of three presentations to engage stakeholders across the dissemination continuum. Researchers, policy makers, and cancer control practitioners will discuss promising approaches and barriers to implementing evidence-based programs and policies. 

In September we explored the “researcher” perspective and this month we turn to policy. A pivotal method of moving research into practice is using the development of policy to enact change. This seminar will describe how experts in the field have leveraged research to make systems and policy changes that improve public health and patient care.  

Dr. Michael Fiore with the University of Wisconsin’s Center for Tobacco Research and Intervention and Ms. Rebecca Kirch with the American Cancer Society’s Cancer Action Network will provide a high level policy overview and discuss their experience as a provider and advocate, respectively, to use data to help drive policy change. Mr. David Rebanal will add to the discussion by describing how data generated at the local level through community based participatory research can be used to inform policy.

Learning Objectives

At the end of the cyber-seminar, participants will be able to:

  • Understand how policy is used as a method to disseminate research into the lives of patients and the public.
  • Describe how tobacco control and pain control legislation came about as a result of new research data.
  • Identify 3 challenges when developing public health policy and how to address those challenges.
  • Discuss how the research methodology can inform a policy agenda at the local level.
