Hali Robinett, M.P.H.

Hali Robinett actively teams with and provides technical leadership and assistance to academic- and community-based researchers, graduate students, federal and state agencies, and non-profit/non-government organizations across the U.S. Affiliated Pacific Islands (USAPI) through her work at the University of Hawaii’s Cancer Research Center of Hawaii (CRCH). Since January 2010, she has served as the Program Manager of the Pacific Cancer Research Group, providing administrative, program and technical support to several cancer health disparities projects, including the NCI-funded University of Guam/Cancer Research Center of Hawaii Partnership (U54) awarded in 2009 and the REACH US CDC-funded Pacific Center of Excellence in the Elimination of Health Disparities awarded in 2007 to the UH John A. Burns School of Medicine’s Department of Family Medicine and Community Health.

From 1995-2010, as Program Director of the National Cancer Institute’s Cancer Information Service (CIS) at the CRCH, Ms. Robinett led a team of Partnership Program coordinators based in Honolulu and Guam. The Partnership team was responsible for providing cancer information, specialized training (e.g. evidence-based programs and practice; cancer clinical trials; cancer 101), and capacity building assistance to organizations and agencies addressing the needs of resource-limited and seriously underserved communities in Hawaii and the U.S. Affiliated Pacific Islands.  

During her 15 years at the Cancer Research Center of Hawaii, Hali has developed specialized expertise in tobacco control, having served as chair of Hawaii’s Tobacco Trust Fund Advisory Board and board member of the Coalition for a Tobacco Free Hawaii.  More recently, she advised the Federated States of Micronesia (Yap, Chuuk, Pohnpei and Kosrae) in their efforts to comply with articles of the Framework Convention of Tobacco Control (FCTC) – a project that will continue through 2011.

Previous contributions to public health in the region have focused on policy development, health promotion and disease prevention through her appointments on:

  • the Governor’s Committee on HIV/AIDS – an HIV/AIDS policy advisory board established by former Governor John Waihee;
  • the Hawaii State Legislature (1992) where she worked as a legislative aide for former Representative Jim Shon and his House Health Committee;
  • and from 1987-89, in the Marshall Islands with the U.S. Peace Corps and the Marimed Foundation. 

A graduate of the UH School of Public Health, Hali served as President of the UH School of Public Health Alumni Association and Program Chair for the Hawaii Pubic Health Association.  She continues to volunteer her time to support community and public health initiatives in Hawaii and the USAPI.
