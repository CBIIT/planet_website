Merrilynn Cushman, M.A.

Merrilynn Cushman is the Tobacco Programs Coordinator for Titusville Area Hospital and a member of the Crawford County Cancer Coalition, both as a tobacco programs service provider and a cancer survivor. The Crawford County Cancer Coalition is a part of the Appalachia Community Cancer Network (ACCN) based at Penn State College of Medicine. The ACCN is a part of the NCI’s Center to Reduce Cancer Health Disparities Community Networks Program.

Ms.Cushman received a B.A. in Speech and Hearing Science and an M.A. in Audiology from The Ohio State University and worked as a Clinical Audiologist in Columbus, OH and Pittsburgh, PA before moving to rural Northwest PA. She embarked on a second career in tobacco programming at Titusville Area Hospital in 2005, where she coordinates Tobacco Programs for portions of 4 counties served by Titusville Area Hospital. In this capacity, she has cultivated partnerships and bi-directional referral networks with physician’s offices, allied health providers, social service agencies, and community groups throughout the region. Her areas of focus are adult tobacco cessation, smokeless tobacco cessation, and post-partum relapse prevention.

She is also an advocacy volunteer for the American Cancer Society’s Cancer Action Network and represented Pennsylvania’s 5th Congressional District as an Ambassador to Celebration on the Hill in Washington, D.C. in 2006. Merrilynn is a 6-year breast cancer survivor.
