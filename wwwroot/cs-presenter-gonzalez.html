Evelyn González, M.A.

For the past 28 years Evelyn González has worked in the public health arena addressing issues of health disparity and health equity. She began her work in the field of reproductive health care and continued on to work in genetics and cardiovascular disease. For the past eleven years she has worked for a program of the National Cancer Institute (NCI). As the former Program Director for the Partnership Program of NCI’s Atlantic Region Cancer Information Service, Ms. González was responsible for overseeing the program implementation for Pennsylvania, New Jersey and Delaware. CIS was known for providing an array of services including technical support in program planning, implementation and evaluation. A unique service provided by the CIS included training partner organizations on the importance using and adapting evidence-based programming. The CIS Partnership Program officially closed in January 2010.

Currently, Ms. González is the Director of Community Programs at the NCI designated cancer center—Fox Chase Cancer Center. She continues to work with disparate populations under the Health Communications and Health Disparities Dept. at Fox Chase. She continues to bring her education and training passion to regional partners, engaging audiences to adopt best practices and helping to them translate science into practice.

Ms. González has co-authored a book entitled Managing a Public Speaker Bureau. The manual provides health and human service providers with systematic guidance and tools to establish, maintain and evaluate an effective speakers bureau.
